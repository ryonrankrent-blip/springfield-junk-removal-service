Springfield Disposal Notes to Know Before Cleanup

City Material Know How guidance lists landfill-banned or specially handled materials such as hazardous wastes, liquids, major appliances, whole tires, waste oil, lead-acid batteries, yardwaste, and asbestos. Springfield also publishes Waste Wizard for item-specific reuse, recycle, and disposal lookups, plus Household Chemical Collection Center guidance for household chemicals by appointment.

Facility acceptance, fees, hours, appointment rules, and item restrictions can change. Customers who plan to self-haul restricted materials should confirm current official guidance before loading a vehicle. These resources are for customer research only. Accepted, Confirm-First, and Not-Standard Materials

The safest way to start is to describe the material before scheduling. Item type, quantity, size, weight, condition, contamination, access, and disposal requirements are confirmed before scheduling.

Commonly Requested

Confirm Before Scheduling

Large logs, stumps, soil, rocks, concrete, brick, roofing, treated lumber, painted wood, refrigerant appliances, TVs and monitors, tires, propane cylinders, batteries, paint, chemicals, pesticides, fuel or oil, unknown liquids, contaminated materials, sharp or bio-contaminated items, unusually heavy items, and mixed loads.

Not Standard Junk Removal

Explosives, ammunition, radioactive materials, medical waste, biohazards, asbestos-containing material, unknown chemicals, active fuel containers, pressurized cylinders, and industrial hazardous waste are not represented as standard junk-removal items.

Confirm-first materials may require special handling, a separate facility, a narrowed scope, or may need to be declined. For chemicals, paint, pesticides, fuels, and similar household hazardous products, use current city or Missouri DNR guidance rather than treating them as ordinary junk.

Quote Request and Confirmation Process

1. Submit a Photo or Description

Share what needs to be removed and include photos when possible.

2. List Item Types and Quantity

Note approximate counts, pile size, or room-by-room scope so the request can be reviewed accurately.

3. Identify Access Limits

Mention stairs, gates, narrow doorways, long carrying distance, parking, or basement and garage constraints.

4. Disclose Special Concerns

Disclose liquids, chemicals, contamination, sharp objects, extreme weight, refrigerant units, or unknown material.

5. Confirm Accepted Scope

Pickup scope, accepted materials, pricing details, and availability are confirmed before anything is scheduled.

6. Schedule if It Fits

There is no obligation to book after receiving quote details. Schedule only after the scope and availability are clear.

Accepted Items FAQ

Do appliances and electronics need confirmation before pickup?

Yes. Appliances and electronics often need confirmation because size, weight, access, condition, refrigerants, fluids, connected utilities, and local disposal or recycling rules can affect scope. Describe the appliance or electronic item and share photos when requesting a quote.

Can yard waste or construction debris be included?

Common yard debris and light remodeling debris can be discussed in a quote request, but material type, size, contamination, and disposal rules still need review. Use the yard waste and construction debris pages for local-rule context, and confirm the load before scheduling.

What information helps most when requesting a quote?

Describe or photograph the items, list approximate quantity, note stairs, gates, narrow access, or long carrying distance, and disclose liquids, chemicals, contamination, sharp objects, extreme weight, or unknown material.
