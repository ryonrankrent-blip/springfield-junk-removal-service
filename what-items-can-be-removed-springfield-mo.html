Common Items That Can Be Removed

Most everyday junk from homes, rentals, and businesses can be removed. If your items fall into the categories below, they are usually a good fit for junk removal.

Furniture

Couches, mattresses, tables, chairs, dressers, recliners, and other bulky pieces.

Appliances

Washers, dryers, ovens, dishwashers, and other household appliances. Ask about units with refrigerant.

Yard Waste

Branches, brush, bagged yard debris, and other non-hazardous outdoor material.

Garage & Shed Junk

Old tools, shelving, bikes, broken equipment, and general garage clutter.

Boxes & Household Clutter

Moving boxes, clothing, toys, decor, and the general clutter that builds up over time.

Light Construction Debris

Wood, drywall scraps, flooring, cabinets, and small renovation cleanup material.

Items That May Not Be Accepted

For safety and legal reasons, some materials are restricted and may not be able to be picked up, or may require specialized handling and disposal. Hazardous materials in particular often need to go through designated local programs rather than standard junk removal.

Items to ask about before pickup

  • Wet paint, stains, solvents, and chemicals
  • Fuels, oils, propane tanks, and similar containers
  • Asbestos and other regulated building materials
  • Medical, biohazard, or contaminated waste
  • Appliances containing refrigerant, which need special handling

This list is a general guide and is not complete. If you are unsure about a specific item, the best step is to describe it when you request a quote so acceptance can be confirmed during the quote process for whether it can be included or where it may need to go instead.

Donation & Recycling Where Available

Not everything that leaves your home has to go straight to the landfill. Where it's available and practical, usable furniture and household goods may be routed to donation, and eligible materials like metal or cardboard may be recycled. Donation and recycling options depend on the item, its condition, and local programs, so they aren't guaranteed for every load — but separating reusable items can help.

Accepted Items FAQ

What items can junk removal companies haul away in Springfield, MO?

Common items include furniture, appliances, yard waste, garage clutter, boxes, general household junk, and light construction debris. If you are unsure about a specific item, describe it in your quote request and acceptance can be confirmed.

Are there items that junk removal cannot accept?

Some items are restricted or may not be accepted, especially hazardous materials such as wet paint, chemicals, solvents, fuels, oils, asbestos, and certain medical or biohazard waste. These often require specialized disposal. Please mention any questionable items when you request a quote.

Can you take appliances that contain refrigerant?

Refrigerators, freezers, and air conditioners contain refrigerant that must be handled under specific rules. Let us know the appliance type so inclusion can be confirmed during the quote process and how it will be handled.

What should I do with hazardous materials you can't take?

Items like chemicals, paint, and other household hazardous waste typically need to go through designated local disposal or recycling programs. Non-hazardous junk can be included in requests; you can route restricted materials to the proper drop-off.

Do you take electronics, TVs, and computers?

Electronics such as TVs, monitors, computers, and printers can often be removed, but some carry e-waste recycling requirements or fees. Let us know the type and quantity when you request a quote so handling can be confirmed during the quote process.

Can you handle a full house or estate cleanout?

Yes. Along with single items, your request can be reviewed for larger projects such as whole-home, garage, basement, and estate cleanouts. Share the rough scope and any items you are unsure about, and quote details are provided after the request is reviewed with quote details.
