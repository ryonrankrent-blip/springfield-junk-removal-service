Confirm Authority Before Requesting a Cleanout

A cleanout request does not determine whether an eviction is complete, whether a unit has been abandoned, or whether left-behind property may legally be removed. The requester is responsible for confirming possession, required notices, court or law-enforcement steps, storage duties, and authority to dispose of each item. When the situation is uncertain, pause the cleanout and obtain legal guidance.

Important: Missouri publishes different procedures that may apply to court-ordered possession and suspected abandonment. This page provides quote-preparation information only and is not legal advice.

Appliances and Electronics

List refrigerators, freezers, TVs, monitors, computers, and connected appliances separately.

Storage and Exterior Areas

Garages, storage rooms, patios, and shared areas require separate access and scope details.

Information Needed for an Eviction Cleanout Quote

Clear details help determine whether the request can be considered and what must be confirmed before scheduling. Photos are especially useful when a unit contains mixed items or access is limited.

Property, scope, and access details

  • Property type, unit number, requester role, possession status, and entry authorization
  • Approximate number of rooms, bags, boxes, furniture pieces, appliances, and electronics
  • Stairs, elevators, gates, parking limits, loading areas, and carrying distance
  • Items that must remain, including owner property, records, keys, appliances, and fixtures
  • Moisture, pests, sharp objects, spoiled food, needles, chemicals, or unknown substances
  • Desired timing, access windows, inspections, repairs, and other turnover work

Materials requiring confirmation

Heavy or oversized items, connected appliances, refrigerators, freezers, TVs, monitors, batteries, paint, chemicals, fuel, oil, propane, medical waste, biohazards, asbestos-related material, and unknown substances require separate review. They may need a different disposal path or fall outside ordinary service scope.

How the Request-and-Confirmation Process Works

  1. Confirm possession, entry rights, and authority to remove the identified property.
  2. Photograph the unit and list items by room or area.
  3. Separate anything that must remain and disclose restricted or unknown material.
  4. Provide access, parking, elevator, gate, and timing details.
  5. Review whether the scope and materials can be considered.
  6. Schedule only after scope, pricing, acceptance, and availability are confirmed.

Quote factors may include volume, weight, labor, carrying distance, stairs, sorting needs, item condition, disposal requirements, and scheduling constraints. There is no obligation to book after reviewing quote details.

Eviction Cleanout FAQ

When can an eviction cleanout request be scheduled?

Scheduling should occur only after the requester has confirmed lawful possession, authority to enter, and authority to remove the identified property. Court orders, notices, storage duties, and abandonment procedures are legal matters for the owner, property manager, or their attorney to verify.

Does a cleanout request include legal or court services?

No. A cleanout request does not include legal advice, court filings, service of notices, law-enforcement duties, decisions about ownership, or confirmation that property is abandoned. Those issues must be resolved before scheduling.

What information helps review an eviction cleanout quote?

Include the property type, unit size, approximate volume, photos, item categories, stairs or elevators, parking and loading access, desired timeframe, and any heavy, sharp, contaminated, chemical, or unknown materials.

Are all left-behind items automatically accepted?

No. Acceptance depends on item type, volume, weight, condition, contamination, access, and disposal requirements. Chemicals, paint, fuel, batteries, pressurized cylinders, biohazards, asbestos-related material, and unknown substances require separate guidance and are not represented as standard junk-removal material.

What affects an eviction cleanout quote?

Quote factors may include volume, weight, labor, stairs, elevator or loading access, carrying distance, sorting needs, item condition, specialty materials, and scheduling requirements. Quote details and availability are confirmed before service is scheduled.
