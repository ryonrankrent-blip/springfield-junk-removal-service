Hot Tub Removal FAQ

How do you remove a hot tub?

Once the tub is drained and utility disconnect status is confirmed, removal scope may include breaking the unit into sections for haul-away. Hot tubs are heavy and bulky, so on-site sectioning is often part of the plan. Labor and disposal are confirmed during the quote process.

Do I need to drain the hot tub before you arrive?

It helps if the tub is already drained, since that saves time. If it still has water, let us know when you request a quote and options can be reviewed during the quote process. Electrical disconnect status should be handled safely, so mention how the tub is wired.

Can you remove a hot tub from a deck or backyard?

In most cases yes. Access matters, so it's helpful to know whether the tub is on a deck, behind a fence, or up a slope, and how much room there is to work. Share those details and scope is confirmed during the quote process the removal and quote it accurately.

Pricing depends on the size of the tub, how it's built, access to the area, and the labor involved in breaking it down. Send a photo and a description of the location for a free quote.
