# Local Entity Eligibility SOP — Pre-Renter and Post-Renter

**Current decision:** Springfield Junk Removal is a lead-generation web property, not a verified operating junk-removal business.

## Pre-Renter Entity Rules

May claim: domain ownership/control, tracking phone, quote-request form, Springfield service-request focus, published informational content, and the fact that requests are reviewed for scope and availability.

Must not claim: storefront, staffed office, local address, employees, crews, drivers, trucks, equipment, licenses, insurance, certifications, operating history, business hours, completed jobs, direct disposal/recycling outcomes, or direct performance of service.

## Post-Renter Integration Rules

1. Verify the operator's legal and public business identity.
2. Verify service territory, services, capacity, hours, direct phone control, required licenses/insurance, and address eligibility.
3. Decide whether the website remains a lead property, co-brands, or becomes operator-branded.
4. Define who owns each listing/account and whether transfer is allowed.
5. Approve website, schema, citation, phone, and routing changes as separate batches.
6. Never merge identities merely to inherit reviews, rankings, or GBP eligibility.

## Citation Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Platform permits the entity type being represented
- [ ] Name identifies the real entity, not a keyword shell
- [ ] Category matches verified services
- [ ] Phone control and forwarding disclosure are acceptable
- [ ] Address is real, eligible, and handled under current policy
- [ ] Hours are supported by the operator
- [ ] Description avoids unsupported performance claims
- [ ] Account owner and handoff path are documented
- [ ] Duplicate/conflict audit completed

## GBP and SAB Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Real operating business makes in-person contact with customers or travels to them
- [ ] Lead-generation-only exclusion does not apply
- [ ] Operator owns/controls the profile and direct customer-facing phone
- [ ] Real operating base exists and is eligible for verification
- [ ] Public address hidden when customers are not served there
- [ ] Service area is accurate and supportable
- [ ] Business name, primary category, hours, website, signage/evidence, and documents agree
- [ ] No virtual office, mailbox, borrowed address, or staged signage

## Required Evidence

- Legal registration and tax/business documents where applicable
- Operator identity and authorization
- Direct phone and domain relationship
- Service-area and capacity confirmation
- Real operating address evidence kept private where policy allows
- Licensing/insurance only when required and genuinely held
- Permanent signage/customer-facing evidence only when actually applicable
- Photos/videos/documents created during ordinary operation, never staged for verification

## Prohibited Representation

- Fake/borrowed address, storefront, staff, vehicle, license, history, hours, review, job photo, document, verification video, or appeal narrative
- Operator facts copied from a renter without written authorization
- Citation or GBP creation solely to make the lead site look like the direct contractor
- Replacement profiles or edits intended to evade enforcement

## Website and Citation Consistency

Audit name, phone, domain, entity description, schema type/address, footer, service area, hours, categories, quote wording, and operator disclosure before any public listing batch.

## Renter Onboarding Inputs

Legal/public name; entity type; owner contact; direct phone; address eligibility; service area; services/exclusions; hours; capacity; licenses/insurance; review assets; category preference; profile ownership; routing approval; data-access permissions; termination/handoff plan.

Any missing or conflicting fact blocks public entity integration until resolved.
