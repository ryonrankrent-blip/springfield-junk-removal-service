### Eligible only after specific operational facts are verified

A GBP may become viable **only if** a verified local operator (renter or contractor) can represent a **real, eligible service-area business** with:

1. A **legitimate physical address** for private verification (not a virtual office, P.O. box, or unrelated home address used deceptively)
2. **Direct control** of the business phone number listed on the profile
3. **Real on-site junk removal operations** in the Springfield service area
4. An authorized representative who is **not** a lead-generation agent acting on behalf of a non-operating web property
5. Ability to pass **postcard, phone, email, or video verification** showing genuine business activity

## Analysis by question

### Does the current site represent a real eligible service-area business?

**No — not as currently operated.**

The production site is a **static lead-generation property** that:

- Captures calls via CallRail and forms via Formspree
- Routes leads to a renter/operator (not yet documented in public listings)
- Does not present a verified operating entity with a confirmable physical presence
- Uses claim-safe copy that avoids operator, crew, truck, license, or insurance claims

Google requires the business represented on GBP to be a **real-world business** that meets customers or travels to them — not a lead-generation website alone.

### Does a physical business location or customer-facing operation exist?

**Not verified in this project.**

- No approved street address exists in repo or documentation
- No storefront, yard, or staffed office is documented
- Conversion verification confirmed **call and form paths**, not a public business location

### Is the current model lead-generation-only?

**Yes.**

Evidence:

- Rank-and-rent architecture (RankRentOS)
- CallRail tracking on a marketing site
- Formspree lead capture
- No operator identity, license, or service vehicle documentation on site
- Google eligibility guidelines explicitly list **“lead generation agents or companies”** as ineligible

Source: https://support.google.com/business/answer/13763036

### Would a renter/contractor need to become the verified operator first?

**Yes — strongly recommended.**

A compliant path would require:

| Step | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 1 | Identify the **actual operating business** fulfilling junk removal requests |
| 2 | That operator becomes the **GBP owner/verifier** (not the web property owner alone) |
| 3 | Business name on GBP must match the **real operating entity** (may differ from site marketing name) |
| 4 | Phone on GBP must be **directly controlled** by the operating business |
| 5 | Website may point to springfieldjunkremovalservice.com only if **owned/managed by the business owner** per Google guidelines |
| 6 | Service-area configuration with **hidden address** if no public storefront |

Without a verified operator, GBP creation is high-risk regardless of SAB settings.

### Risks of fake address, virtual office, coworking, or unrelated home address

| Approach | Risk | Google policy |
|---|---|---|
| Fake / invented address | **Critical** — suspension | Prohibited |
| Virtual office / mail drop | **Critical** — suspension | Explicitly ineligible unless staffed by your team during business hours |
| Coworking desk without signage + staff | **High** — suspension | Requires permanent signage, your staff on site, customer visits |
| Unrelated home address (not yours) | **Critical** — fraud | Prohibited |
| Operator's real home address (hidden SAB) | **Medium** — viable only if legitimate | Allowed for true home-based SAB with address hidden from public |
| P.O. box | **Critical** — rejection | Not acceptable |
