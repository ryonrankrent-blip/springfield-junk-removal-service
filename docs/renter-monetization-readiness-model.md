# Renter & Monetization Readiness Model — Springfield

**Status:** Internal preparation only; no renter outreach or routing authorized.

## Candidate Score (100 Points)

| Dimension | Points | Evidence |
|---|---:|---|
| Reputation and complaint risk | 20 | Reviews, public records, consistency, unresolved patterns |
| Capacity and responsiveness | 20 | Coverage, hours, phone response, scheduling capacity |
| Service/territory fit | 15 | Springfield coverage and matching service mix |
| Lead handling and close process | 15 | Intake, follow-up, estimates, CRM/ledger willingness |
| Digital weakness/opportunity | 10 | Can benefit without destructive brand conflict |
| Entity/compliance fit | 10 | Legitimate operator, accurate facts, listing/routing readiness |
| Commercial fit | 10 | Job value, margins, trial and flat-fee ability |

Reject or pause for deceptive practices, serious reputation risk, inability to serve, refusal to track outcomes, demands for misleading claims, or requests to route before approval.

## Lead Economics Inputs

Qualified leads; answered rate; appointment rate; estimate rate; close rate; average job value; gross margin assumption; service mix; cancellations; duplicates; spam; wrong-area calls; response time. Mark every assumption separately from verified data.

## Model Readiness

| Model | Use | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Limited proof-of-life trial | Preferred first step | Approved candidate, duration/cap, routing, quality ledger, privacy, termination |
| Exclusive flat monthly rental | Preferred after proof | Stable qualified volume, operator satisfaction, pricing rationale, attribution |
| Pay per lead | Avoid by default | Exact qualified-lead definition, dispute rules, dedupe, compliance, invoicing |
| Revenue share | High verification burden | Closed-job reporting, audit rights, attribution, payment timing, privacy |
| Hybrid | Later option | Base fee plus clearly defined performance component |

## Trial Structure

Define candidate, territory, services, start/end, lead cap, exclusivity, response standard, lead handling, recording/consent, routing rollback, qualification definition, dispute window, reporting, no-guarantee statement, and post-trial decision.

## Lead-Routing Approval Checklist

- [ ] Owner explicitly approves operator, number, date, services, territory, and rollback
- [ ] Operator identity/capacity/reputation verified
- [ ] CallRail/Formspree/data access and privacy approved
- [ ] Test call/form and failure path approved
- [ ] Current routing captured for rollback
- [ ] No website/listing/entity changes bundled without separate approval

## Lead-Quality Ledger

Date/time; source; call/form ID; service; city; new/duplicate; answered; qualified; spam/vendor/wrong number; appointment; estimate; won/lost/pending; estimated/actual value; response time; operator notes; dispute; recording consent/access; QA reviewer.

## Attribution & Revenue Verification

Use stable tracking identifiers, dedupe rules, source timestamps, operator disposition, and periodic reconciliation. Revenue share requires documented closed-job evidence and privacy-safe audit rights. Never infer revenue from call duration alone.

## Decision Thresholds

- **Not ready:** tracking, eligibility, routing, or candidate unresolved.
- **Trial ready:** verified candidate plus repeat qualified leads and approved rollback/reporting.
- **Flat-fee ready:** trial demonstrates reliable value and operator responsiveness.
- **Scale ready:** economics remain stable across multiple reporting periods without compliance drift.
